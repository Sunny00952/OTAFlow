Bridging the Gap: A Hierarchical Framework for Cross-Modal Style Modeling in Expressive TTS

Abstract

 Natural language prompts offer a highly flexible interface for expressive text-to-speech (TTS), yet cross-modal gaps and one-to-many mapping problem hinder reliable style control. To address this, we propose OTAFlow, a novel hierarchical cross-modal style modeling framework. First, optimal transport alignment, contrastive learning, and multi-task supervision are jointly optimized to construct a unified style space that preserves instance-level correspondence and expressive factor separability. Second, a conditional flow matching module is introduced to capture the residual modality gap, explicitly addressing the one-to-many challenge by generating diverse acoustic style embeddings from a single prompt. When plugged into a downstream TTS backbone, evaluations show that our method substantially outperforms baseline models in fine-grained style retrieval and synthesized style accuracy, effectively delivering diverse and controllable expressive speech.
